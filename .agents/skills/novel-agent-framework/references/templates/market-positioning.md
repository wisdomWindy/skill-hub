# Market Positioning Template

## Required Artifact Path

- `docs/novel-projects/<novel-id>/artifacts/market-positioning.md`

## Required Sections

- project identifier
- raw concept summary
- delivery format
- target platform or channel
- genre and subgenre lane
- target readership
- age band and content boundary
- word-count strategy
- chapter-length strategy
- update cadence or delivery cadence
- comparable titles
- comparable-title takeaway
- market promise
- hook density expectation
- emotional payoff expectation
- style and diction expectation
- commercial differentiation
- red lines and exclusions
- premise tension with market fit
- downstream constraints for structure and pacing

## Section Guidance

### `delivery format`

At minimum, state one of:

- serialized web novel
- commercial trade novel
- genre series installment
- novella
- short story

If the work does not fit these categories, describe the closest operational equivalent so later stages still inherit pacing and structure constraints.

### `target platform or channel`

Describe where the work is expected to live or be judged, such as:

- web-serialization platform
- print-first commercial publishing
- digital-first storefront
- contest / award submission
- newsletter / subscriber fiction

This section must influence later choices about:

- chapter rhythm
- hook density
- exposition tolerance
- ending cadence

### `target readership`

Describe the intended reader cohort with enough specificity to drive writing decisions:

- age range
- reading habit
- genre familiarity
- tolerance for slow burn vs immediate hook
- appetite for complexity
- preferred emotional register

Avoid "everyone" or other non-operational audience labels.

### `word-count strategy`

State:

- total target range
- minimum viable range
- preferred long-form ceiling
- whether the story is intended as stand-alone, expandable stand-alone, or series-capable

### `chapter-length strategy`

State:

- preferred chapter length range
- acceptable variance
- whether cliffhanger endings are standard, occasional, or rare
- whether scenes tend to be compressed or expansive

### `comparable titles`

List at least 2-3 comps or comp-combinations. For each one, describe:

- what it contributes as a comp
- what should be borrowed
- what should be explicitly avoided

### `market promise`

Answer:

- What emotional or narrative promise does this project make to its target reader?
- What would make the target reader feel cheated?

### `hook density expectation`

State:

- opening hook speed
- chapter-end hook expectation
- midpoint escalation expectation
- climax intensity expectation

### `commercial differentiation`

Describe what meaningfully separates this work from its comps:

- concept twist
- tonal mix
- character angle
- structural novelty
- thematic sharpness
- market crossover

### `downstream constraints for structure and pacing`

This section must translate positioning into actionable writing constraints for later stages.

At minimum, specify:

- exposition tolerance
- acceptable setup length before disruption
- subplot weight
- romance / mystery / action / intrigue ratio when relevant
- pacing floor that later chapter and scene planning must not violate
